\section{Introduction}

Welcome to Latexifier, a fast and minimal home for all of your LaTeX writing.
Edit the source on the left and watch the PDF update on the right. Inline
math like $E = mc^2$ renders just as you would expect, and you can flip on
the visual editor to shape equations the way you would in Desmos.

\section{Equations}

The quadratic formula gives the roots of $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:
\[
  \sin\left(\theta\right)+\cos\left(\Omega\right)=\tan\left(2\Phi\right)
\]

A familiar identity from analysis is
\[
  \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n!} = e,
\]
and Euler tied together five constants in one line:
\[
  e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0.
\]

\section{Wrapping Up}

That is the whole idea: write quickly, see results instantly, and reach for
the visual editor whenever an equation is easier to build by hand than to
type. Happy writing!
